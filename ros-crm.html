Concept / Brand — not a hierarchy level

Wendy's, IHOP, Applebee's are brand labels — a field on the Site object, not a separate record or hierarchy level. One operator can have multiple concepts. Each concept gets its own Site and Deal, not its own Company.

Three real configurations — same model, different permutations

Simple operator

No holding group · 1 operator · 1 concept · 1 site

e.g. Lenz Enterprises LLC operating Cheba Hut — 7 locations. One Company, one Deal, one Site.

Multi-concept operator

Holding group → 1 operator · 2 concepts · 2 sites

e.g. Good Times Restaurants, Inc. operating Good Time Burgers (27 locs) + Bad Daddy's (37 locs). One Company, two Deals, two Sites.

Complex group

Enterprise → multiple legal entities · multiple concepts · multiple billing sub-entities

e.g. Hamra Enterprises → Hamra Chicago LLC + Wendy's of Missouri Inc. + Wendy's of New England LLC. Three Companies, N Sites, 4 billing sub-entities.

Decisions needed

These must be resolved before configuration can begin. Sorted by urgency.

Critical — blocking architecture and integration

Integration architecture: Option A or Option B?

Option A: Aggregate fields directly on the Company record (MRR, location count, products). Fast to implement, no new objects. Fragile — sync relies on name matching, breaks with any rename. Technical debt.
Option B: Site as a custom HubSpot object, associated via rosnet_site_id. Correct architecture. Data flows cleanly at site level. More complex — requires whiteboard session with Jack + Josh verification. Recommended. Everything else depends on this decision.

Fix site_id in Maxio — move from subscription to customer level

The rosnet_site_id currently only exists at the subscription level in Maxio — not at the customer/billing entity level. This means you cannot search for a billing entity by site name. Any HubSpot ↔ Maxio sync will fail without this fix. Must be resolved with the Finance and tech team before any integration work begins.

Contract renewal tracking — what is the infrastructure?

The first contract with a term limit (1-year and 3-year options) was sent on May 28, 2026. All previous contracts were evergreen — no renewal tracking was ever needed. No system, reminder, or automation exists today. Proposed: Contract as a custom HubSpot object with end date field and automated reminders. Who owns the design and configuration of this?

Companies owned by departed employee — reassign or not?

A former team member owns 876 Company records in HubSpot. 0 are active clients. 493 have no lifecycle stage. She has been on indefinite leave for 4 months with no official status update. This is a legal situation — cannot reassign without explicit approval from leadership. A decision is needed before cleanup can proceed.

High — blocking cleanup and configuration

New concept for same legal entity: New Business or Expansion?

When a current client adds a new concept (e.g. Sun Holdings already has Bar Louie on ROSnet and now wants to add Popeyes), what deal type should Sales use? The taxonomy is ambiguous today — some reps mark it as New Business, others as Expansion. This affects pipeline reporting, commission calculation, and how the deal type field is configured in HubSpot.

HubSpot auto-creates a Company from every inbound email — disable?

An active HubSpot automation creates a new Company record for every inbound email — including vendors, payroll companies, and irrelevant contacts. This is inflating the company count daily and polluting the CRM. Options: disable the automation entirely, or create a "Vendor / Other" lifecycle stage to filter these out from client views.

Statements of Work — where are they tracked?

The CS team sends SOWs (professional services) to existing clients. This is real revenue that does not appear in any deal or pipeline in HubSpot today — completely invisible to leadership. Should SOWs live in a separate CS pipeline, as a custom deal type, or as a custom object? Needs a decision before the CS pipeline is designed.

Should billing sub-entities become a HubSpot object?

Billing sub-entities currently live in Maxio only. Some clients (like Hamra with 4 billing entities) have complex billing structures. Today, sub-entities are being confused with Companies — creating duplicate records. Should they become a custom object in HubSpot, or remain Maxio-only and be represented via fields on the Site object? Requires data team input.

Deal distribution rule between sales reps

When an inbound lead comes through the website form, there is no formal rule for which rep takes it. The decision is made case by case. As the team grows, this is unsustainable. A distribution rule is needed — by geography, by segment, by concept type, or by rotation.

Medium — needed for naming and data cleanup

ICP Score criteria — what defines High, Medium, Low?

The ICP Score (High / Medium / Low) needs to be defined before it can be configured in HubSpot. Possible criteria: location count, concept type (QSR vs casual dining), ownership model, geography, or a weighted combination. Currently scored via Apollo export but invisible in HubSpot — needs validation with Sales leadership.

Naming conventions — Company, Site, Contact

Deal naming is confirmed: [Operator] — [Concept]. All other naming conventions are still TBD: Company (legal name vs DBA), Site ([site_id] — [descriptive name]), Contact (First Last (Role)). These must be agreed before deduplication begins — you cannot deduplicate with inconsistent names.

Does the site name go in the deal name or as a separate field?

Sales reps search by site name (e.g. AZCHEBA, DOHERTY) — not by legal operator name. The confirmed deal name convention is [Operator] — [Concept]. Should the site name also appear in the deal name, or only as a separate searchable field? Both must be accessible to reps. Tom: "I know what we refer to them as in Rosnet."
